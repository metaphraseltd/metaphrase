Tamil (தமிழ்) is one of the world's oldest classical languages, with a literary tradition spanning over 2,000 years. Tamil is written in its own distinctive script — a syllabic abugida with 12 vowels, 18 consonants, and a further 216 combined characters — that is entirely different from the scripts used in North Indian languages such as Hindi, Urdu, or Punjabi. Tamil linguistic expertise requires specific training and cannot be substituted by a translator of other South Asian languages.

Understanding Certified Translation

What Is a Certified Tamil Translation?

A certified Tamil translation is a complete, word-for-word English rendering of your original Tamil document, accompanied by a formal statement — known as a certificate of accuracy — signed by the translator. That certificate confirms three things: that the translator is competent in both Tamil and English, that the translation is a true and complete representation of the original document, and that the translator accepts professional responsibility for its accuracy.

At Metaphrase, our certificates include the translator's full name, CIOL professional credentials, contact details, signature, date of certification, and ICO registration number. This is the document UKVI, the Home Office, and UK courts rely on when accepting translations.

Tamil is a complex classical language with a unique script and a rich literary register distinct from everyday spoken Tamil. Machine translation handles Tamil poorly compared to major European or North Asian languages, and generalist South Asian language translators who work in Hindi, Urdu, or Bengali cannot translate Tamil — it is a Dravidian language entirely unrelated to the Indo-Aryan family. A certified translation from a qualified Tamil specialist is not only required by UKVI — it is the only way to ensure accuracy.

Two Major Communities

Sri Lankan Tamil and Indian Tamil:
One Script, Two Contexts

The UK's Tamil community comes primarily from Sri Lanka and from Tamil Nadu in India. Both communities use the same Tamil script, but their official documents differ in format, language of administration, and institutional conventions.

இலங்கை தமிழ்

Sri Lankan Tamil

Jaffna · Batticaloa · Colombo Tamil community · UK diaspora

Sri Lankan Tamils are the largest Tamil community in the UK, with significant populations in London, Birmingham, and other major cities. Sri Lanka's civil war (1983–2009) and its ongoing aftermath have generated a substantial community of refugees, asylum seekers, and settled migrants. Sri Lankan Tamil documents present a distinctive characteristic: official civil documents are typically issued bilingually, in both Sinhala (the majority language) and Tamil (an official minority language), often with parallel columns or sections.

Sri Lankan birth certificates, marriage certificates, and identity documents are issued by the Sri Lanka Civil Registration Bureau (formerly the Registrar General's Department) in a standardised format. The Tamil sections use formal written Tamil with specific Sri Lankan administrative terminology. Documents from the Northern and Eastern Provinces — Jaffna, Batticaloa, Trincomalee, Vavuniya — may differ in format from those issued in Colombo and the South, particularly older documents issued during and after the civil war period.

  • Sri Lankan birth certificates (bilingual Sinhala/Tamil)
  • Marriage certificates from Jaffna, Batticaloa, Colombo
  • Sri Lankan National Identity Card (NIC) details
  • Civil war-era and post-war Northern Province documents
இந்திய தமிழ்

Indian Tamil (Tamil Nadu)

Tamil Nadu · Puducherry · Indian Tamil diaspora in UK

Indian Tamil documents originate primarily from Tamil Nadu — India's southernmost state and the cultural homeland of the Tamil people — as well as from Puducherry. Indian Tamil documents are issued by Tamil Nadu state government authorities. Birth certificates, marriage certificates, school records, and university degrees from Tamil Nadu are typically issued in Tamil with some English elements, following Indian civil registration formats that differ from Sri Lankan documents.

The Indian Tamil community in the UK has grown significantly with the expansion of skilled worker visas and the IT sector, with Tamil Nadu producing many of the UK's technology professionals. Indian Tamils also form a significant part of the NHS workforce. Documents required for UK spouse visas, skilled worker visas, student visas, settlement, and citizenship applications from Indian Tamil nationals are issued under the Tamil Nadu and central Indian government systems.

  • Tamil Nadu birth and marriage certificates
  • Indian passports with Tamil name entries
  • Tamil Nadu university degrees and transcripts
  • Documents for skilled worker and spouse visa applications

Both Sri Lankan and Indian Tamil documents use the same Tamil script. If your document contains both Tamil and Sinhala text (common for Sri Lankan documents), we translate both sections. If you are unsure about your document's origin or format, send it to us and we will confirm at no charge.

Document-Specific Expertise

Tamil Documents We Translate

Each document type has its own conventions. We know them across Sri Lanka and India.

Tamil Birth Certificate Translation

Sri Lankan birth certificates are issued by the Civil Registration Bureau in a bilingual Sinhala/Tamil format. The certificate contains the child's full name, parents' names, date and place of birth, registration number, and the Divisional Secretariat or Grama Niladhari division responsible. Tamil birth certificates from the Northern and Eastern Provinces follow the same national format but may have been issued by local authorities during and after the civil war period, when central government administration was disrupted.

Indian Tamil birth certificates from Tamil Nadu are issued by the municipal corporation, panchayat, or hospital authority under the Registration of Births and Deaths Act. Older Tamil Nadu birth certificates may be entirely in Tamil; more recent ones typically include English alongside Tamil. We translate all formats, including older handwritten or typed documents with faded text.

Tamil Marriage Certificate Translation

Sri Lankan marriage certificates are issued under the Marriage Registration Ordinance in a bilingual Sinhala/Tamil format. Tamil marriages in Sri Lanka may be registered under the General Marriages Ordinance (civil marriage) or under Kandyan law (for Kandyan Sinhalese), Muslim law, or the Jaffna Matrimonial Rights and Inheritance Ordinance — the last of which is specific to the Tamil community of the Jaffna Peninsula and has its own distinct legal framework and document format.

Indian Tamil marriage certificates from Tamil Nadu are issued by the District Registrar or the Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Department depending on the form of registration. Temple marriages may require an additional religious certificate. We translate all Sri Lankan and Indian Tamil marriage documentation, including the specific Jaffna matrimonial law certificates that are unique to the Sri Lankan Tamil community.

Sri Lankan Tamil Asylum Document Translation

Sri Lankan Tamils constitute one of the long-established asylum-seeking communities in the UK, with claims relating to the civil war period (1983–2009) and its aftermath in the Northern and Eastern Provinces. Documents submitted in support of asylum claims may include civil registry records from Jaffna, Batticaloa, or Trincomalee districts, court documents, medical records showing injuries or treatment, police station records, letters from religious or community figures, and evidence of property loss or displacement.

We understand the political and historical context of Sri Lankan Tamil asylum claims thoroughly — including the documentation challenges arising from the war period, the limited administrative function of the Jaffna District Secretariat during certain conflict periods, and the ongoing human rights concerns in post-war Sri Lanka. Our translations of asylum evidence are accepted by the Home Office, the Asylum and Immigration Tribunal, and immigration solicitors.

Accepted by: Home Office, Asylum & Immigration Tribunal, UKVI. Sensitive and confidential handling guaranteed.

Tamil Academic Qualification Translation

UK ENIC, professional regulatory bodies, and UK universities require certified translations of all overseas academic qualifications. Degrees, transcripts, and mark sheets from Sri Lankan universities — University of Jaffna, University of Peradeniya, University of Colombo, Eastern University — are issued in Sinhala and/or Tamil. Indian Tamil academic documents from institutions such as Anna University, University of Madras, Madurai Kamaraj University, and their affiliated colleges are issued in Tamil and/or English.

Many Tamil professionals — particularly IT engineers, medical doctors, dentists, pharmacists, nurses, and accountants — require certified translations of their qualifications to work in the UK. Avoid Costly Mistakes

Common Problems With
Poor Translation Providers

Choosing the wrong Tamil translation provider can cost you time, money, and — in immigration cases — your application.

Using Machine Translation

Google Translate and similar tools cannot produce certified translations. UKVI will automatically reject any translation produced by AI or machine translation — regardless of how accurate it appears. The absence of a human translator's certification is grounds for refusal.

Using a Bilingual Friend or Family Member

However fluent, a translation produced by someone without professional qualifications cannot be certified for official purposes. UKVI specifically prohibits translations by family members. Submitting such a translation can result in immediate rejection.

Unfamiliarity with Tamil Document Formats

Tamil documents from Sri Lanka and India differ significantly in format, civil registry structure, and administrative vocabulary. A translator unfamiliar with these differences may produce an inaccurate translation that UKVI or courts reject.

Missing Certificate of Accuracy

Some providers deliver translations without the required certificate — or with a generic unsigned statement. Without a properly formatted and signed certificate, your translation is unusable for official purposes and will be rejected by UKVI and courts.

Missed Deadlines

Large online agencies often miss promised delivery times. Missing an immigration appointment or court filing deadline because your translation was late can have serious consequences. With Metaphrase, you deal directly with the translator and know exactly when your document will be ready.

Hidden Costs

Some providers quote a low headline price and then charge separately for the certificate of accuracy, formatting, or urgent processing. Our quotes are fixed and all-inclusive — the certificate is always included, with no add-on fees.
